Research Overview

My research focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence and economic analysis, developing innovative computational methods to understand complex economic phenomena and improve decision-making processes.

Through advanced machine learning techniques, econometric modeling, and data science approaches, I investigate how AI can enhance our understanding of economic behavior, market dynamics, and policy outcomes. My work bridges theoretical economic research with practical applications in forecasting, policy analysis, and business intelligence.

The research portfolio spans multiple domains including predictive modeling, natural language processing for economic texts, behavioral economics through computational methods, and the development of AI-driven tools for economic analysis and decision support.
