About Me

I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Economics and Econometrics at the University of Manitoba, building upon a diverse academic background including degrees from the University of Antwerp and the Catholic University of Bukavu. My multidisciplinary perspective and international experience inform both my teaching and research.

As a sessional instructor at Booth University College, I design engaging, data-driven learning experiences in Microeconomics and Applied Finite Mathematics. My research leverages quantitative methods and machine learning to tackle real-world economic challenges, with a focus on policy relevance and innovation.

Research Highlights

My core research interest lies in the innovative application of quantitative methods to economic problems. Currently, I am focused on:

Integrating AI and Machine Learning in macroeconomic and financial modeling.

Econometrics, including causal inference, time series analysis, and microeconometrics.

Monetary policy, DSGE models, and growth dynamics.

Financial economics, encompassing risk modeling and asset pricing.
