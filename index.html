Biography

Frederic Mirindi is a PhD Candidate in Economics & Econometrics at the University of Manitoba. He is a leading researcher specializing in artificial intelligence applications in economic analysis. With extensive experience in machine learning models for economic forecasting and policy analysis, he has pioneered innovative approaches to understanding economic phenomena through computational methods.

Courses I Teach at Booth University College

As a Lecturer at Booth University College, I teach foundational courses that bridge economic theory with practical applications and research methodologies.

Introduction to Microeconomics

ECO110

Fundamental principles of microeconomic theory including supply and demand, market structures, consumer behavior, and firm decision-making. Students learn to analyze individual economic units and their interactions in various market environments.

Market Analysis Consumer Theory Price Elasticity Market Failures

Applied Mathematics

MAT150

Mathematical methods and applications in economics and business contexts. Coverage includes calculus, linear algebra, optimization techniques, and statistical foundations essential for economic modeling and analysis.

Calculus Optimization Linear Algebra Statistical Methods

Research Methods

BHS200

Comprehensive introduction to quantitative and qualitative research methodologies in economics and social sciences. Students learn data collection, analysis techniques, research design, and academic writing skills.

Data Collection Statistical Analysis Research Design Academic Writing

Business Information Systems

BUS270

Fundamental concepts of information systems in business contexts, focusing on how technology supports organizational strategy, decision-making, and operations. Students explore system design, data management, and the role of IT in modern organizations.

Information Systems Data Management IT Strategy Business Processes
