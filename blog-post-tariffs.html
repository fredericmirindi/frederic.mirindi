U.S. Tariffs: Global Shockwaves and African Trade Implications

Posted on April 18, 2025 | Category: Trade Policy, Global Markets

5. Behind the Tariffs: Rationale, History, and Global Risks

Source: The Week, April 2025 Edition

This editorial breakdown from The Week highlights the justification behind U.S. tariffs under Trump, notably arguing that America’s trade partners imposed higher average tariffs than the U.S. It emphasizes historical grievances and perceived asymmetries in WTO rules, particularly with countries like China, India, and members of the EU.

Economists cited in the article warn that such measures mirror the protectionist Smoot-Hawley Act of the 1930s, which escalated into a global trade war and prolonged the Great Depression. Moreover, a rising dollar—paired with global retaliation—could trigger inflation and financial turbulence across U.S. markets.

Understanding these differences clarifies why U.S. officials push for "reciprocity," but also why economists caution against blanket tariffs. The structure of global trade is not only about tariffs, but also about comparative advantage, product specialization, and geopolitical leverage.
