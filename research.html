Research

Choices under uncertainty.

My doctoral research investigates how agents make decisions when outcomes are uncertain, drawing on random expected utility theory, game-theoretic foundations, and modern econometric tools. I also work on development economics, sovereign debt, and applications of AI to economic forecasting.

Themes

Decision Theory

Random expected utility, stochastic choice, and identification of preferences from observed behaviour.

Game Theory

Strategic interaction, information design, and equilibrium selection.

Development

Sovereign debt, IMF programs, and the macroeconomic history of crisis episodes.

Econometrics & AI

Machine learning for economic forecasting, causal inference, and geospatial analysis.
